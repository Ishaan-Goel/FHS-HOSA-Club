Member Requirements

Member Obligations:


  1. 1. Attend meetings. (3 absences limited to school-related activities and must be excused)

  2. 2. Earn a minimum of 25 hours through participation in club activities. 10 of these hours must be from a health-related facility. If you participate in a community service project, you will also be awarded "service hours" that can be used to meet your graduation requirements.

  3. 3. Pay annual dues, field trip, and project fees on time.

  4. 4. Participation in HOSA competitions at the regional level is required (state and national levels if qualified.)

  5. 5. Exhibit good character and have school records free of disciplinary action.

  6. 6. Must be an active participant in the Health Fair preparation Committee of your choice.

  7. 7. Must attend at least one guest lecture during the year.

General Meetings:


  1. 1. General meetings are held once whenever stated on the calendar so please check the calendar frequently. This is non-inclusive of committee meetings that are mandatory for members to remain active in the club.

  2. 2. A student will receive an hour for each entire general meeting he/she attends. A student who does not attend or who leaves early for another extracurricular event will not receive credit.

  3. 3. A student will attain hours for each committee meeting he/she attends. On the sign-in sheet, members MUST write their sign-in and sign-out time to be awarded the appropriate amount of club hours.

  4. 4. A student who misses more than three general meetings (excused or unexcused) will be placed on probation and possibly be ineligible for an HOSA cord at graduation.

Graduation Recognition:


HOSA Cords: Senior members in good standing are eligible for an HOSA honor cord. To receive an honor cord, a student must:
  1. 1. Attain 3.5 or greater unweighted GPA in Health Science Classes

  2. 2. Complete 30 participation hours for each year he/she is in HOSA. 15 of these hours must be earned through a health-related facility.

  3. 3. Complete 80 community service hours by graduation

  4. 4. Attend all general meetings with the exception of one per semester

  5. 5. Participated in at least 2 HOSA fundraising events and 2 community service events during senior year

  6. 6. Participated in a HOSA Regional Competitive Event

  7. 7. Participated in Health fair through the committees

  8. 8. Attend a guest lecture

  9. 9. Satisfied the terms to be removed from member probation (if applicable)

Probation:

Probation = a temporary disciplinary period when failure to attend or participate in HOSA activities results in the loss of in-club participation hours. (deduction of 5 hours for each probationary period) Grounds for probation include the following:
  1. - Failure to attend general meetings.

  2. - "No Showing" at an event previously signed up for.

  3. - Failure to complete a minimum total of 25 participation hours per year.

  4. - Incidents of disciplinary action including (but not limited to) cheating, suspension, or consistent school rules violations.

  5. - Not being an active committee member in activities such as health fair.

  6. - Failure to attend a guest lecture.

HOSA members will be notified of their probation via email.

Consequences:


  1. Failure to complete 25 participation hours for each year he/she is a member of HOSA. (30 if you are a senior)
    • Student will not be eligible for a cord at graduation and/or recognition at HOSA end of the year banquet/celebration.
    • Possible removal from the club if the student is inactive for a prolonged period of time.
  2. Failure to maintain good character.
    • If a student violates this standard (i.e. documented cheating, suspensions, forging of signatures, referrals, and/or general documented misconduct), he/she must appear before the HOSA advisors for disciplinary review. Possible reprimands include: additional mandatory HOSA service hours during the next semester/year, denial of a cord at graduation, and/or dismissal from HOSA.
